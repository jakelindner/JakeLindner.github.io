What is a Data Analyst?

Jake Lindner

A Data Analyst is a professional who takes raw data and conducts meaningful analysis to inform better business decisions. Data analysis typically moves through the following phases:

  • Identify the business question you'd like to answer
  • Collect the raw data
  • Clean the data
  • Analyze the data
  • Interpret the results of the analysis
  • Present your analysis to stakeholders

Once these steps are complete, the analyst moves to the storytelling piece. The analyst will then create data visualizations illustrating the story/solution they found in their analysis.

Different Types of Data Analysis

Descriptive Analysis

A descriptive analysis goal is to tell you what happened. Typically statistics are used to describe or summarize quantitative data. For example, a descriptive analysis could show profit broken down by each ice cream sold.

Diagnostic Analysis

A diagnostic analysis goal is to tell you why it happened. For example, an ice cream store had the most sales in June because it was the first month of summer.

Predictive Analysis

A predictive analysis goal is to use data from the past and form projections about the future. For example, the ice cream store will now predict that next year in June will be their highest profit period.

Prescriptive Analysis

A prescriptive analysis goal is to use all the strategies above to give recommendations to the business. For example, the analysis may recommend hiring more staff and buying extra inventory to capitalize on the high-selling period.
